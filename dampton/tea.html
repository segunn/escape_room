Escape from Dampton Court

Looking around, this is clearly the location for the famous Dampton Court all-you-can-eat afternoon tea.

Three small tables must have been carved by a master craftsman, but their recent maintenance has been… innovative. Held together by Blu Tack and Polyfilla, it looks like they might collapse if too much weight is placed on any of them. The cake stands have spaces for baked goods, but they’re empty.

The coffee table is well supplied with light reading materials.

A cardinal named Thomas Wosley,
Was drinking his tea – or was he?
If in the wrong cup
He’ll refuse to sup
He’ll sit and he’ll stew and sulk crossly

Though known for having the blues,
The cardinal’s pick of the hues,
It has to be said
Can only be red
His dress sense offers clear clues

In September 1521, Suffolk led his household, family, and friends to war in France. His expedition was co-ordinated with Bourbon’s rebellion. Unfortunately his enthusiasm for biscuits was irreparably damaged when he saw Bourbon had failed completely: his conspiracy had crumbled and his army dissolved. Henceforth, Suffolk’s preferred cocoa-based snack was to be the brownie, even leading to his nickname “Charles Brownie” at court.

Memo: Guest Preferences

Henry wants Jane at his right hand. Wolsey refuses to sit at the same table as Anne Boleyn. Anne of Cleves is partial to a blueberry muffin, while Catherine of Aragon will only eat tea loaf. Catherine Parr’s favourite snack is the Parrty Ring

On Seating Plans

Never seat a lady next to her husband’s other wife.
To seat two guests with the same name on the same table risks embarrassing confusion, avoid this at all costs.
All suitable guests will know it’s polite to take the slice of cake nearest to you.

On Crockery

All the teacups on a table should have matching colours.
Anne should have a spotted teacup but Catherine must have a striped teacup.
It is unbecoming for a man to use a patterned teacup.

Pastime with good company
I love and shall until I die;
Grudge who will, but none deny,
So give me please my pecan pie.
For my pastance
Hunt, sing, and dance.
My heart is set:
All goodly sport
For my comfort,
Who shall me let?
